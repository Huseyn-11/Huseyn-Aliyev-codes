To Kill a Mockingbird-To Kill a Mockingbird (1960) by Harper Lee is a Pulitzer Prize-winning Southern Gothic novel set in 1930s Alabama. It follows young Scout Finch as her lawyer father, Atticus, defends a Black man falsely accused of rape, exposing themes of racial prejudice, innocence, and moral integrity. The story is a bildungsroman heavily influenced by Lee’s own childhood.
